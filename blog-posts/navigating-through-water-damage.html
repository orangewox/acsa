Navigating Through Water Damage

A Comprehensive guide on what to do after experiencing Water Damage and why to call a Restoration Company

‍

Water damage can indeed be a homeowner's worst nightmare. It brings an immediate disruption to daily life and, if not addressed properly, can lead to long-term structural problems. With Acceder Building Solutions, you never have to face this daunting challenge alone. As experts in building science, we have the expertise and resources to help you navigate through the complexities of water damage restoration. 

Understanding the Complexities of Water Damage

Water damage can be deceptive. It starts subtly but can quickly spiral out of control if not promptly addressed. Various incidents can lead to water damage, including: 

  • A plumbing leak or pipe burst
  • Flooding from an appliance leak (Dishwasher, Washing Machines, Refrigerator)
  • A leaking roof
  • Buildup of high relative humidity over time

‍

The damage can range from mild staining and dampness to severe damage compromising structural components. Also, there is always the possibility of rapid mold growth and even dangerous electrical issues.

‍

When you first identify the telltale signs of water damage, such as discoloration, peeling paint, persistent dampness, or an unusual musty smell, it's crucial to act swiftly. Water damage issues can escalate rapidly, leading to potential health risks from mold growth and further structural damage if left untreated.

‍

The Immediate Steps to Take When You Have Water Damage

Prioritize Safety 

The first step should always be to ensure everyone's safety. If the water damage is severe, it may have compromised the structural integrity of your home, or there could be electrical hazards. If you have any doubt, evacuate your home and call for professional help.

‍

Stop the source of water

If it's safe and possible, identify and stop the source of the water to prevent further damage. This could involve shutting off the water supply if a pipe burst or plumbing leak is the cause.

‍

Document the Damage

Begin taking photos and making detailed notes of all visible damage. This documentation will be invaluable when it comes to filing an insurance claim with your homeowner’s insurance company.

‍

Commence Cleanup and Drying

When it is safe to do so, start drying out the affected area as much as possible to prevent mold growth. Please be aware that without professional equipment, your efforts may not fully mitigate the damage.

‍

Recognizing When to Call a Restoration Company

Minor water damage can be handled independently. Although, if you are inexperienced and have any doubts, it is highly recommended to call in professionals. We work with water damage situations regularly giving us a level of expertise that you can rely on for proper mitigation. It’s important for homeowners to recognize when it is advised against homeowners undertaking independent water damage mitigation measures, such as the following:

Extensive Damage

If the water damage affects a large area or has seeped into walls, floors, or furnishings, it's time to call in the professionals.

‍

Unidentified Water Source

If you can't locate or halt the water source, it is imperative that a professional team help trace it and prevent further damage. There are situations where a leak may not be active until the right conditions, such as a heavy enough rainfall. If the water source is not traced accurately, this can result in further damage to your property in the future.

‍

Contaminated Water

Due to the associated health risks, a homeowner should never take measures to mitigate if the water comes from sewage or any other potentially hazardous source. It is advised to call in a professional immediately.

‍

Mold Growth

When there is active mold growth within your home, it must be addressed properly to prevent further contamination of other areas of your home. When removing mold, it is very easy to cause mold spores to become airborne resulting in cross contamination of other areas of your home. If you see signs of mold, you should contact a professional restoration company immediately.

‍

Insurance Claims

Processing insurance claims related to water damage can be complex. A professional restoration company can provide the proper documentation and assistance needed. At Acceder, we work on your behalf directly with your insurance company to provide an efficient claims filing process.
